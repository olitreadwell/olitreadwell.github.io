Hi there!

__I graduated from Dev Bootcamp last week! It was fantastic, but it's taken a long time to get here.__

Before I came to [Dev Bootcamp](http://devbootcamp.com), I worked with a fantastic community of people in Melbourne, Australia. While there I worked with Melbourne's leading early-stage technology venture capital firm, [Adventure Capital](http://adventurecapital.vc/). The Partners (Thanks Darcy and Stu!) and their team (there's too many of you, but you know there's a warm spot in my heart for each of you) jolted my ambition to a next level. The [York Butter Factory](http://yorkbutterfactory.com/)  community, founded by Adventure Capital, gave me the foundation to do great things.

From there I went on to one of the hardest learning experiences I had before Dev Bootcamp. Working with [MMGN.com](http://mmgn.com/) as their Operations Manager was going from the heat of moving to a new country to the fire of a startup. Roy, Gaetano, and Ben made me feel welcome. They were flexible in understanding that I was doing my best. At MMGN.com, I learned what it takes for me to succeed and developed great relationships with my teammates.

It's from my time in Australia that I knew that I wanted to become a developer.

Looking back nearly a year later, blows me away. Entering into Dev Bootcamp I had strong ideas about what I should expect from this program. Graduating now, those expectations were swept away. As the staff and boots at Dev Bootcamp will say again and again, when you graduate you don't do so as a Rails Developer.

Dev Bootcamp threw me into the unknown again and again. The number one skill that I gained from Dev Bootcamp is how to pick up an unfamiliar piece of technology. I can learn about it and implement its core features within hours of introduction. Did I also learn many other things? Of course! Dev Bootcamp demonstrated the importance of git versioning, object oriented methods, READING THE ERROR MESSAGES and documentation, agile and test driven development. I worked on soft skills like having tough conversations, working with widely different people, and how to be kind to myself. Sure, I worked with Ruby, Ruby gems, git, jQuery, AJAX, RSpec, Jasmine, Capybara, Shoulda-Matchers, SQL & PostgreSQL, Sinatra & Rails, CSS3, Sass, and Compass. Those were icing on the cake.

[I am further delving into the world of technology products](http://bit.ly/olitreadwell-linkedin). I came to Dev Bootcamp to give myself the skills to build fantastic products alone and with a team. I leave here raring to enter a junior developer role, ready to build my career with the support of the wonderful folk at Dev Bootcamp.
