Imagine you sit down on your next birthday and pull out a paper.
You write at the top, “Bucket List.” You list the goals you wish to achieve. What
are the things you’d do if there weren’t any restrictions? It might be silly.

It might be extravagant.

1. Fly a plane

2. Summit Mt. Everest

3. Visit every continent

4. Road trip Australia

You stare at the list. It’s daunting. These tasks are HUGE.

How will you ever complete them?

That’s the gut reaction. These are giant to-do’s, on your daily to-do list. You know, the one that never seems to end?

Wheeeeeoowww…

Let’s take a step back.

### The Backwards Bucket List
The backwards bucket list is not about pining after something unaccomplished. It’s not about counting down the days.

But…they go hand-in-hand.

Imagine you sit down on your next birthday and you pull out a paper. You write at the top, “Backwards Bucket List.” You list your greatest accomplishments.

What's the one moment you look back on with pride?

Perhaps your most recent accomplishment is forgiving a friend. Maybe it _is_ climbing the Himalayas (You
overachiever you!). Big or small, write it down.

Look at that list! It's immense; it's wonderful. Throw some gold stars on it; coverit in glitter. Take a photo of it. Be proud of it.

### Why do this?
Writing your Backwards bucket list helps you in 2 ways.

#### I. In crisis
When you suffer from “What am I doing with my life?” or “What have I done with my life?”, the backwards bucket list will give you perspective.

This is a list of your accomplishments. This isn’t a resumé; _life isn’t about your resumé_. It’s about life. Remember that.

Remember you’ve come a long way already and you’re not stopping.

#### II.The bucket list
Pull out that daunting bucket list. Stare it down.

Now look back at your Backwards bucket list. Admire it. You have the ability to overcome so much.

These lists go hand in hand. They sit next to each other.

Why? Doesn’t that bucket list appear more achievable, more doable? Don't you think with the right focus, you can cross another off your bucket list soon? Find the easy one.

Now, search Backwards bucket list for the hardest one. The accomplishment that hurt most or took the most time.

If you put the effort into the easiest item on your bucket list that you did to accomplish the hardest on your Backwards bucket list, you know it’d be done quickly.

Don’t forget these great things. You've done much that you should be proud of.

So take 25 minutes and right out that Backwards bucket list. Be grateful for the things you've accomplished.
