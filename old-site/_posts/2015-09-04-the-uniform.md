I keep playing around with this idea that it would be fun to create a uniform for myself. Something that I was able to just wear all the time without feeling weird about it. Something that would fit for all occasions.

I've seen great examples of this. A good example being a teacher I know at Dev Bootcamp Chicago, who wears white t-shirts and jeans. Another engineer that wears a white collared shirt and blue jeans all the time. I even saw a post from a gent awhile back about wearing all black for a year.

One of the best known examples of this is Steve Jobs. Sure, he pulled it off. I guess it kept him well dressed. I always wonder though... how many different activities did he use it for? Or did he have another uniform for some other activities? I can't imagine he hit the treadmill in a black turtleneck and blue jeans. What do I know?

My argument against it is that I have two ideas of things I'd like to wear all the time.

## 1. Work & Play

<em>A long sleeve button-up with a nice pair of jeans and comfortable brown dress shoes.</em>

Wearable at work, wearable <em>basically</em> anywhere. The primary deficiencies of this attire being that, I don't <em>need</em> to dress up for my job and Austin, Texas is <strong>HOT</strong>.

Otherwise, a good option, though it will take a little more maintenance. I won't want to wear an un-ironed shirt or one with pit stains.

## 2. Working Out

<em>A well-fitting solid color workout shirt without design, with a tank top underneath, workout shorts or nice sweatpants, and running shoes.</em>

Again, I'm lucky enough that this <em>could</em> be work appropriate, though I'm not sure I'd feel comfortable in it.

It's not happy hour appropriate. It's not date appropriate. It's not even the "dress for the job you want" appropriate, unless that job is being a personal trainer.

Austin is casual enough that if I were to wear this out for casual beers, people wouldn't look at me too strangely... Otherwise, it's only going to fit this single function rather than a multitude of functions.

Another plus, is that this is suitable for all seasons, especially Austin's summer heat/humidity.

I got onto this idea when I realised, that I had been filling up my walk-in closet, out of capacity rather than necessity. All the random clothes that I have that I never wear. Things that I bought and don't like anymore, or have found there's no situation I need them for.

I enjoy being able to dress up and change my outfit. To try out different looks. It's a fun transformation from gym rat to programmer. From going out with friends to lazing around the house.

----------------------------------------------------------

In the end, perhaps this is more a mental experiment leading me to declutter, than to create a Jobs-ian uniform.
